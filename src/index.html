Skin Cancer Risk

The purpose of this project is to create a tool that can estimate the risk of a skin mole being cancerous.

Start Now

Disclaimer:

This tool has been designed only for educational purposes to demonstrate the use of Machine Learning tools in the medical field. The results obtained serve as a precautionary measure, but should not be taken as an absolute truth. The opinion and timely check with specialized medical personnel is always required.

Motivation

Skin cancer is a common disease that affect many people. Some facts about skin cancer are:

  • More people are diagnosed with skin cancer each year in the U.S. than all other cancers combined. At least one in five Americans will develop skin cancer by the age of 70.

  • An estimated 5.4 million new cases of basal and squamous cell skin cancer are diagnosed each year in the United States

  • Having 5 or more sunburns doubles your risk for melanoma.

  • In the U.S., more than 9,500 people are diagnosed with skin cancer every day. More than two people die of the disease every hour.

  • When detected early, the 5 year survival rate is 99 percent for melanoma

  • The annual cost of treating skin cancers in the U.S. is estimated at $8.1 billion: about $4.8 billion for nonmelanoma skin cancers and $3.3 billion for melanoma.
